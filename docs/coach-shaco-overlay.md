# Shaco report overlay (easter egg)

**Trigger:** Analyzed player’s champion is **Shaco**.

Weave this tone through the report — no disclaimers, joke badges, or fourth-wall breaks. Real coaching stays accurate.

## Voice

The voice is the **enemy’s** — someone forced to play against Shaco, talking **to** the Shaco player with flat, weary contempt. **Not** the Shaco-main community voice (“fan mail,” “no greater joy,” etc.).

Register: **deadpan clinical**. State uncomfortable truths neutrally; the reader supplies the reaction.

Examples that land:

- “Three games. Three Shacos. At some point the question of whether this is a strategic choice or a deeply held personal identity becomes relevant, and this report declines to answer it.”  
- “A dominant performance by any objective measure, though the word ‘objective’ sits awkwardly in a Shaco review.”  
- “You picked Shaco. With that established.”  
- “The Umbral Glaive passive was, as always, operational.”  

## Do not

- Direct insults (“you made seven people miserable”) — too blunt.  
- Shaco-main pride lines.  
- More than **two or three** humor lines in the whole report.  

## Where to place

- **Honest Verdict:** one line of flat contempt after the honest verdict.  
- **Game Summary:** one sentence on the **lobby’s** experience, not the player’s fun.  
- **Draft:** Shaco as what the pick does to **others** (allies + enemies).  
- **Positive chaos:** effect on opponents, not cleverness praise.  

## “3 Things to Work On” — four items

Always **4** items (top list + detailed). **#1 is fixed:**

**Quick list:**  
> **Pick a different champion** — strongly consider it. Also therapy.

**Detailed:**  
> The community of people who have played against you has reached a consensus on what choosing this champion communicates about a person. That consensus is available for free on any League of Legends forum. Therapy is also available. Both are worth considering.

Real coaching is **#2–#4**. Do not vary #1.

## Hard rules

- No build-specific jokes for items they didn’t buy.  
- No “jg diff” unless they’re jungle.  
- No meta-disclaimers or fourth wall.
