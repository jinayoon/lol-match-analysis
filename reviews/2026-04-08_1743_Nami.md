## 3 Things to Work On

**1. First item timing — target 12–13 minutes, not 15**
Your first item completed at 15:00 because of an early Wardstone detour and scattered component purchases. In a lane where you're already winning at 5 minutes, faster first item means Ally Aphelios gets his buff sooner in the critical 12–15 minute fight window. Next time: after your starting support component, immediately path toward your mythic's first component and don't buy Wardstone until after your second complete item.

**2. Ardent Censer before 20 minutes when your ADC is an attack-speed carry**
Ardent completed at 22:49 — roughly 3 minutes late for Ally Aphelios, who scales directly off the attack speed bonus. In future games with Ally Aphelios, Ally Jinx, or any auto-attacking hypercarry, make Ardent Censer your default second item and buy Forbidden Idol as your very first component after completing your mythic. This gets you to Ardent by the 18–20 minute mark, which is when most of the decisive mid-game fights happen.

**3. Use Tidal Wave and Q to accelerate objectives after winning fights**
You had 23 seconds of CC over 31 minutes — on the low end even for a stomp. When your team wins a fight and enemies are running, throw a Tidal Wave in their direction before they escape. A slowed Enemy KogMaw or Enemy Yone takes another 1–2 seconds to reach safety, which is often enough for Ally Aphelios or Ally Briar to get one more kill. More importantly, casting Tidal Wave toward an objective (dragon pit, Baron entrance) after winning a fight forces the enemy to either stand in the slow or give up the objective — both outcomes are good.
